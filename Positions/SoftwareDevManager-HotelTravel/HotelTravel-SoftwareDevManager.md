## Context

HotelTravel.com was an online hotel booking platform based in Phuket, Thailand. The core product was a hotel reservation system that aggregated rates and inventory from multiple categories of hotel rate providers (e.g., GDS systems, direct hotel connections, third-party aggregators, or similar). This kind of multi-provider integration is technically complex: each provider has different data formats, availability models, rate structures, cancellation policies, and API behaviors that must be normalized into a coherent booking experience.

The Software Development Manager role combined formal people management of a large team with deep hands-on technical involvement in architecture, R&D, and individual mentorship — a hybrid engineering leadership role rather than a purely organizational management position.

## Technical Work

### Booking Engine Overhaul

Led and contributed directly to a full overhaul of the underlying back-end booking engine. This was a complex system responsible for integrating hotel rate and availability data from multiple categories of providers. The overhaul required re-architecting how data from disparate sources was normalized, combined, cached, and served to the booking layer — a technically demanding integration problem at the core of the product.

### Multi-Provider Rate Integration

The booking engine's central challenge was managing data from heterogeneous hotel rate providers. Different provider categories (e.g., GDS, direct hotel APIs, aggregators) vary significantly in data format, update frequency, rate structure, and availability semantics. Designing a clean integration layer that abstracted these differences required both architectural judgment and deep domain understanding.

### Testing and Quality Policy

Designed and implemented TDD and QA policies covering both automated and manual testing for new software components. The goal was to ensure that each new component met quality standards independently and integrated correctly into the overall booking system. This was a department-level initiative, not just personal practice.

### Mentorship

Mentored individual developers across multiple dimensions:

* Software micro-design: design patterns, clean interfaces, responsibility separation
* New technologies: mobile development
* Engineering best practices: efficiency, security, and scalability

The mentorship was individual and hands-on, consistent with the broader hands-on leadership style of the role.

### Operations and Process

Ran Scrum-based development operations for the department: facilitated daily standups, managed Jira for sprint planning and issue tracking, and served as the primary communication channel between the engineering team and the executive suite. Translated technical progress, risks, and decisions into executive-level reporting while also translating business priorities back into actionable engineering work.
