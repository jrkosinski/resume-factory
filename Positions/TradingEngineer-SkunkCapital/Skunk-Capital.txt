## Context

Skunk Capital wanted an automated system to exploit arbitrage opportunities between cryptocurrency futures and spot markets.

This was a cash-and-carry strategy: the system identified cases where futures and spot markets diverged enough to justify opening paired positions, after accounting for costs and execution risk. The bot then automatically executed both legs, tracked the position, and unwound it when conditions justified closing.

This was the user's first trading bot, but it became a substantial production system. It reportedly performed very successfully, and the client had enough confidence in it to test with approximately six-figure capital allocations.

For external resumes, the safest phrasing is:

> Built a profitable production crypto arbitrage bot used with six-figure test capital allocations.

Avoid claiming exact ROI, annualized return, total P&L, or AUM unless those numbers are later recovered.
