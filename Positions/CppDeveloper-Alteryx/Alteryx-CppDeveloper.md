## Context

Alteryx is an enterprise data analytics platform used by business analysts, data scientists, and IT teams to automate data preparation, blending, and analysis through visual, drag-and-drop workflows — without requiring users to write code. It supports ETL pipelines, predictive modeling (including R and Python integration), and geospatial analysis.

The core of the platform is a C++ back-end engine that executes these workflows at runtime. This engine is the performance-critical layer under all user-facing functionality: it processes data, runs transformations, executes tool logic, and manages the execution pipeline. Defects in the engine directly affect the reliability and correctness of data analytics workflows for enterprise customers.

Working on the engine meant operating at the lowest layer of a widely-deployed commercial product — where bugs had real customer impact and where correctness, performance, and cross-platform consistency were all first-class concerns.
