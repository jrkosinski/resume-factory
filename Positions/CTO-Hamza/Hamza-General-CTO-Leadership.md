## Context

Hamza was created to decentralize e-commerce for sellers by allowing them sovereignty over their data, brands, products, and identity through Ethereum, Optimism, and EVM L2 infrastructure.

The company’s flagship marketplace was a live reference implementation of the broader ecosystem idea: sellers could list products, buyers could shop through familiar commerce flows, and crypto-native payment and escrow infrastructure operated underneath the marketplace.

The role required both executive ownership and direct engineering participation. The team was small, the budget was constrained, and the system crossed several difficult domains: e-commerce, Web3 identity, crypto payments, cross-chain settlement, Solidity escrow, backend infrastructure, seller tooling, and production operations.
